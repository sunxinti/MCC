   - **Function:** Connects the lateral ventricles to the third ventricle, allowing CSF flow between them.

   - **Function:** Allows CSF to flow from the fourth ventricle to the subarachnoid space.

   - **Conclusion:** Unlikely to result in isolated dilation of the left lateral ventricle.

5. **Cerebral aqueduct (Option 5):**  
   - **Function:** Connects the third ventricle to the fourth ventricle, facilitating CSF flow between them.  
   - **Relevance:** Obstruction would typically cause dilation of both the lateral and third ventricles, not just one lateral ventricle.  
   - **Conclusion:** Does not match the unilateral ventricular dilation seen in this case.
